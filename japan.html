Japan

Why I love Japan

Japan has always been one of those places I've dreamed of visiting. Everything about it just feels so fascinating and unique. The first thing that draws me in is the blend of ultra-modern cities and deeply rooted traditions. I want to walk through the busy streets of Tokyo, surrounded by towering skyscrapers, crazy-cool shops, and high-tech everything. But then, I also want to experience the calm, serene side of Japan—like exploring the beautiful temples and gardens of Kyoto or soaking in an onsen with a view of Mount Fuji. The food is another huge reason I want to go. Authentic ramen, sushi, tempura—just tasting Japanese dishes right where they originated sounds like heaven. Plus, I've heard the street food in places like Osaka is unbeatable. And of course, Japan is famous for its festivals. I’d love to experience a traditional matsuri with all its colorful floats, dances, and local food. Their culture and hospitality are something I’ve always admired. The people are known for being polite and welcoming, and everything seems so organized and efficient. Even their public transportation is world-class, with trains that are super clean and punctual. Another thing I would add to all of these is all the Anime things that you can see all throughout the busy side of Japan, and I am all for it.

How to get there?

Getting to Japan from the Philippines is straightforward, which is nice. There are direct flights from Manila to major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, and Nagoya, which usually take about four to five hours. It’s really convenient, and once you arrive, getting around Japan is super easy thanks to their impressive train and bus systems.
