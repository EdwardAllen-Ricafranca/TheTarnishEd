Canada

Why I love Canada

Canada is a nation of stunning contrasts, from the cosmopolitan urban centers of Toronto and Vancouver to the rugged, untouched beauty of the Rocky Mountains and the icy majesty of the Arctic. Tourists from around the globe are drawn to Canada for its diverse landscapes, vibrant cities, and welcoming atmosphere. Visitors rave about the natural beauty of Canada. Banff and Jasper National Parks in Alberta are celebrated for their breathtaking mountain views, pristine lakes, and incredible hiking opportunities. The Niagara Falls, straddling the border of Canada and the United States, remains one of the most iconic natural attractions in the world, captivating millions of tourists each year. Canada’s cities offer something for everyone. Toronto, the nation’s largest city, is known for its multicultural atmosphere, iconic CN Tower, and vibrant arts scene. Vancouver, situated between the Pacific Ocean and the Coastal Mountains, offers outdoor adventures, scenic beauty, and a laid-back lifestyle. Montreal and Quebec City, with their rich French heritage, charm visitors with their European-style architecture, delicious cuisine, and lively festivals. Beyond natural beauty and urban sophistication, Canada is renowned for its friendly, polite citizens and high quality of life. It consistently ranks as one of the best countries to live in, offering safety, healthcare, and education to all. This welcoming atmosphere extends to tourists, who often comment on the kindness and helpfulness of Canadians. Personally, I am drawn to Canada’s combination of stunning natural landscapes and world-class cities. I would love to explore the Rocky Mountains, hike through lush forests, and experience the vibrant cultural life of Toronto and Montreal. The idea of witnessing the Northern Lights in the Yukon or enjoying the serenity of Banff’s turquoise lakes excites me tremendously. Canada is a country that offers experiences for all kinds of travelers, from adventurers seeking the great outdoors to culture enthusiasts craving rich history and diverse cuisine. It’s a destination that promises unforgettable memories and countless stories to share.

How to get there?

To get to Canada from the Philippines, you’ll most likely be flying out from Manila’s Ninoy Aquino International Airport or Mactan-Cebu International Airport. The fastest way is to book a direct flight to Vancouver with Philippine Airlines, which takes around 13 hours. But if you’re headed to cities like Toronto or Montreal, you’ll probably have to catch a connecting flight through major hubs like Hong Kong, Seoul, or Taipei. Make sure your passport is valid and you have a Canadian visa—usually a Visitor Visa if you're going for tourism. Once you’ve sorted that out, just book your flight, pack for Canada’s weather, and prepare for a long but comfortable journey. Canada’s incredible landscapes and vibrant cities will make the long flight totally worth it.
